**Heading:**
By the Time You Know, It's Already Cost You

**Body:**
A missing tooth enters a crusher. A buried drill rod is scooped into a haul truck. A boulder too large for the grizzly hits the feed.

### Missing Tooth Detector

A camera mounted on the excavator watches the bucket continuously. The moment a tooth is broken or missing, the operator receives an instant audible alert — no connectivity required. Stakeholders are notified in real time when connected.

One missing tooth left undetected can cause catastrophic damage to crushers and conveyors downstream. Atlas Merlin catches it at the source, before it costs you.

*Coming soon: predictive wear-rate tracking, so you'll know when a tooth is approaching failure before it happens.*

### Foreign Object Detector
**Badge:** World First

**Kicker (bold):** Nothing like this exists anywhere else.

A camera mounted on the excavator watches the ground and bucket ahead. When it spots something that shouldn't be there — a drill rod, an old tooth, a cable, buried debris — the operator is alerted instantly.

Today, foreign objects are only caught by sensors further downstream, often after the damage has begun. Atlas Merlin detects them at the point of excavation, in real time, before they enter the processing chain.

### Boulder Monitoring

A camera positioned above a grizzly crusher monitors incoming material. It estimates boulder size in real time and flags anything too large — or anything that shouldn't be there, like a foreign object that made it through.

When connected to the crusher's e-stop system, intervention is automatic. No operator reaction time required.

The same system analyses fragmentation patterns, giving your blasting team the data to understand whether charges are producing the right material size — closing the feedback loop between blasting and processing.

### Haul Road Monitoring

A single camera on a haul road can do more than you'd expect.

Originally deployed to monitor pantograph compliance — identifying which trucks are connecting to overhead electrical trolley lines and which aren't — the system expanded to provide a full picture of road activity. Vehicle counts by type, congestion tracking, and real-time dust and visibility monitoring that triggers water truck dispatch when conditions deteriorate.

One camera. Multiple operational insights. Each one feeding decisions that reduce cost, fuel usage, and risk.
